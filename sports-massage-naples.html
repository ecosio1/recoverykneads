What is Sports Massage?

Sports and deep tissue massage combines targeted therapeutic techniques to address muscle tension, improve circulation, and enhance recovery. This focused approach is effective for athletes, active individuals, or anyone seeking deeper muscle work to alleviate chronic tension and restore optimal function.

This isn't just about relaxation—it's about optimizing your body's performance, preventing injuries, and helping you bounce back stronger after every workout. Whether you're a weekend warrior or training for a marathon, sports massage is an essential tool in your fitness arsenal.

Sports Massage for Every Stage

Different training phases and activities require different massage approaches. We tailor each session to your specific needs and timing.

Pre-Event Massage

Timing: 15 minutes to 2 hours before activity

Purpose: Warm up muscles, increase circulation, and prepare your body for peak performance. Light, stimulating techniques that energize without causing fatigue.

  • Increases blood flow and muscle temperature
  • Improves flexibility and range of motion

  • Enhances mental focus and readiness

Post-Event Massage

Timing: 30 minutes to 2 hours after activity

Purpose: Aid recovery, reduce muscle soreness, and help your body transition back to normal function. Gentle techniques that promote healing.

  • Reduces delayed onset muscle soreness (DOMS)

  • Supports natural healing processes

Maintenance Massage

Timing: Regular sessions during training cycles

Purpose: Keep muscles in optimal condition, prevent injuries, and address imbalances before they become problems. Deeper, more thorough work.

  • Maintains muscle flexibility and health
  • Identifies and addresses muscle imbalances
  • Prevents overuse injuries
  • Supports consistent training performance

Rehabilitation Massage

Timing: During injury recovery phases

Purpose: Support healing from injury, restore normal function, and safely return to activity. Gentle, targeted techniques that promote tissue repair.

Your Questions Answered

Sports Massage FAQ

Get answers to common questions about sports massage and how it can benefit your athletic performance and recovery.

Do I need to be a professional athlete to get sports massage?

Not at all! Sports massage benefits anyone who is physically active, from weekend warriors to fitness enthusiasts. If you exercise regularly, play recreational sports, or have an active lifestyle, sports massage can help you perform better and recover faster.

How often should I get sports massage?

This depends on your training intensity and goals. Active individuals often benefit from sessions every 2-3 weeks, while athletes in heavy training may need weekly sessions. We'll help you determine the optimal frequency for your needs.

Is sports massage more painful than regular massage?

Sports massage typically uses deeper pressure than relaxation massage, but it should never be painful. We work within your comfort level and adjust pressure based on your tolerance and the goals of your session.

When is the best time to get sports massage?

Timing depends on your goals. Maintenance massage is best on rest days or light training days. Pre-event massage should be done 15 minutes to 2 hours before activity. Post-event massage is most effective 30 minutes to 2 hours after intense activity.

What should I bring to my sports massage appointment?

Wear or bring comfortable athletic clothes. Bring a water bottle to stay hydrated, and let us know about your recent training, any soreness, and your upcoming activities so we can tailor the session appropriately.

Can sports massage help prevent injuries?

Yes! Regular sports massage helps identify muscle imbalances, tight areas, and potential problem spots before they develop into injuries. It also maintains muscle flexibility and optimal function, which are key to injury prevention.
